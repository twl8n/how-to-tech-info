
#### Frigidaire Professional FPIC3077RF Inductive Cooktop

This document created/revised 2026-04-14 April 14, 2026

- 30 inch, ADA Compliant Induction Cooktop
- 4 Elements 
- PowerPlus Induction Technology 
- SpacePro Bridge Element
- Knob Controls
- Stainless Steel (front trim and knobs)
- Beeps once when temp knobs are moved. Cannot be disabled, as far as I know.
- Cooling fans have a buzz
- LED indicates power level when on
- LED HE indicates when a burner may be hot from contact with a pan
- LED colors are kind of a dim, dark blue/navy 
- Retail price $2500. Price I paid $680. 
- The FPIC3077RF will probably be discontinued soon. I expect parts to be available (nearly) forever.

My cooking impression is 5 stars. Cooking is very quiet. Some induction cooktops make an occasional squeek or chirp. This FPIC3077RF cooktop is absolutely silent. (Aside from when the fan kicks on.) At the lowest setting, the induction cycles. Most people wouldn't notice. If you watch very carefully at a very low simmer, you'll notice the boil increases and decreases on a 1 second or 1.5 second cycle. Subtle. My other cooktop (Ikea single burner) does this on power levels 1, 2, and 3, and far more noticable. Probaly something all inductive cooktops do.

There are two cooling fans. The fans get 3 stars, or less. The fans are temperature sensing and each fan only runs when required. Sadly, the fans are buzzy sounding. The noise isn't too irritating when the drawers under the cooktop are closed. If I had paid the $2500 retail price I'd be pretty darned upset with the buzzing fans. Why aren't all cooling fans quiet? I had a whisper quiet 5 inch CPU cooler on my AMD Opteron in 2001, and now 25 year later Frigidaire can't make a quiet fan? Clearly, they aren't trying.

Wiring is 4 wire, 220 volt, 2 hot, 1 neutral, 1 ground. Cable from the cooktop is metal clad, 1 1/4 diameter. Junction boxes with 1 1/4 knockouts are not common in home centers. Wire clamps/hubs for 1 1/4 are also not common.

Miniumum current draw: 0 amps measured with a Klein amp clamp. Zero on both hot wires and on the neutral, so
it really is zero. My guess is that the knob control needs zero current draw. Knob controls are simpler.

Other units with touch countrols may require some current for the microcontroller that senses the touch panel.

As of April 5, 2026, the Frigidaire FPIC3077RF is listed as "Out of Stock". I purchased this new unit from Amazon via a third party reseller for $680. Frigidaire retail $2,499. I suspect this unit will soon be discontinued. I also expect Frigidaire to carry parts for many years in the future. Remember, even if you buy a currently-produced unit, in a few years it will also be discontinued. Buy a brand/manufacturer that will continue to carry parts for many years (or basically forever).
